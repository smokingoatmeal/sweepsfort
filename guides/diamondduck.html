The Basics

There are 7 tiers of Diamond Duck status, as follows:

Blue -> Yellow -> Pink -> Orange -> Red -> Midnight -> Radiant

Each tier is significantly more difficult to unlock compared to the previous tier, and comes with a larger one-time discount coin pack. (*One time each month that you unlock it, that is!)

For most players, going after the blue and yellow tiers each month will provide the best balance of effort and reward. After yellow, you will need to dedicate some serious time and effort (and likely purchase additional coin packs) if you want to progress to the pink and higher tiers.

Diamond Duck status is achieved by playing games on Luckyland Slots. Both gold coin play and sweeps coin play progress your status. Given that it takes quite a lot of play to complete a tier, it generally makes the most sense to do this with gold coin play.

What is critically important to understand, is that bet size appears to have no impact on your progress. Your advancement is driven purely by the number of spins, bets, or plays that you make. What does this mean? It means that it makes sense to do the lowest bet size possible, for efficiency (to waste as little gold as possible.)

As you play games, note the diamond icon in between your duck avatar and the “Buy” button at the top of the screen:

This diamond will sparkle as you play, and it will gradually fill up with a color that represents the tier you are currently working toward. Each time the diamond completely fills up, the percentage shown underneath it will increase by 1.

When you hit 100%, you have reached that tier, and unlocked your reward pack:

Congratulations, you did it!!!

Recommended Games

For achieving Diamond Duck status, not all games are created equal.

You are going to want to conserve your gold, so that you can advance to the highest tier possible. Also, when you are done for the month, the gold you have managed to keep will be extremely valuable when you repeat this process next month.

Currently, there are 3 games that seem to work best:

  • Luckyland 7s
  • The Great Pigsby
  • Starspell

Next, we will go over the pros and cons of using each of these games.

Luckyland 7s (LL7 for short)

Minimum Bet Size: 250 GC
Speed: Fast

This one’s an old-school 3-reel slot. It may not look like much, but it’s an absolute killer for Diamond Duck. The minimum bet size is fairly low at 250 gold per spin, which is good for our purposes. While variance is always a factor, LL7 tends not to burn gold fast. Also, LL7 does not require constant user interaction – it will not stop after a free spin, or wait for you to click.

Based on our experience, by playing LL7 it will take you about 16–18 hours to achieve the first tier (Blue Duck), and about 90–94 hours to achieve the second tier (Yellow Duck.)

The Great Pigsby (Pigsby for short)

Minimum Bet Size: 10,000 GC
Speed: Medium

Pigsby is a newer offering on Luckyland. It’s a very solid game, and some players are experimenting with using it to chase Diamond Duck status. Like LL7, Pigsby is generally great at conserving your gold. It also features an unlimited autoplay (just click the + in the autoplay menu until the spins count changes to an infinity symbol) and the game will continue to run for hours without player intervention.

There are two significant drawbacks to Pigsby that we should point out, however:

  • The minimum bet size is 10,000 GC. This is not ideal for our purposes, and it does likely mean that you will want to have more gold to start with, to be able to weather any long losing streaks.
  • Pigsby is a game with a relatively high frequency of free spin rounds. When you trigger the 10 free spins, you are losing time, because unpaid spins don’t count toward your diamond duck progress. So every time a free spins round triggers, the whole time those 10 spins are running, that is dead time.

We have not tested enough with Pigsby yet to quantify how long it takes to reach various tiers of Diamond Duck using this game. However, we have heard enough positive feedback from players using it to justify including it here.

StarSpell

Minimum Bet Size: 1,000 GC
Speed: Breaking the sound barrier

StarSpell is an interesting game. It features a burst mode, which goes insanely fast. Starspell is by far the fastest option we are aware of for earning Diamond Duck tiers. However, there are a couple of drawbacks to using StarSpell.

  • StarSpell will burn through gold fairly quickly, unless you get lucky. There is significant volatility at play here!
  • StarSpell is incredibly popular and there are sometimes issues with the game timing out. This is especially pronounced earlier in a month, when many players are all trying to play this game to tier up in Diamond Duck.

I only recommend StarSpell in two specific situations. First, if you have an absolutely ridiculous amount of gold (we have seen players with 100 million gold for example) – then you may simply not care that much about efficiency, and in that case StarSpell makes plenty of sense.

Second, if you want to pursue the higher tiers of Diamond Duck, a game with burst mode becomes nearly the only viable way to do so. You simply can’t complete enough spins with LL7 or Pigsby in a month, to hit the highest tiers. Pink is technically possible to achieve with LL7, but it will become a full-time job. Orange and up are likely not possible without a burst mode game.

The amount of gold you get will be determined by your level (a separate system from the Diamond Duck program.) You can check your level by clicking on the duck icon at the top left of your screen in Luckyland:
